Chickasaw Nation, Multimedia Internship, Summer 2026

Himak Nitak & The Chickasaw News Segment

This summer I'm interning as a multimedia intern with the Chickasaw Nation, learning every stage of production alongside industry professionals, from casting and writing through filming and editing. As a Chickasaw citizen, working within my own community has shaped how I think about storytelling in ways I'm still processing.

My twin Ainsley and I are producing two original projects: Himak Nitak (Chickasaw for "present day stories") is a podcast about what it means to be an indigenous artist today, covering identity, representation in media, and the gap between how Native people are portrayed and how we actually live. Alongside it, we're writing and producing a comedic Chickasaw News Segment, collaborating with the Toksali SMART summer youth program to give young Chickasaw students hands-on production experience.

In production, Summer 2026

About

I'm a junior at SCAD studying film and television with a minor in casting. Somewhere along the way I got obsessed with the part of filmmaking that nobody talks about: finding the right people to tell stories with.

I currently co-head the SCAD Casting Office, spending most of my time helping directors do exactly that, across thesis films, VFX productions, and original projects. This summer I'm interning with the Chickasaw Nation, making things with my own community. Next summer, I hope to be in a professional casting office learning from people who have been doing this for a long time.
